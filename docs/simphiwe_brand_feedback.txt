Good thinking bro, you're on the right track. Few things though:

The categories you picked as proxies — health food, fruit & veg, supplements, fast food — those are more grocery shoppers than beauty buyers. If we're building an audience for Revlon or Eucerin, we need people who actually shop in beauty/pharmacy, not people buying bananas.

Better proxies would be:
- Clicks, Dis-Chem, Sorbet, Edgars Beauty shoppers (the actual stores that sell these products)
- In categories like Pharmacy, Health & Beauty, Personal Care
- And specifically people who spend a lot there, not just once-off. So filter for share of wallet > 50% at those stores

The other thing is you're trying to build one audience but actually each brand needs a different cut:
- Revlon = mass market makeup, so mid-income women, younger
- Eucerin / La Roche-Posay = premium dermocosmetics, higher income, wider age range
- Vital = vitamins/supplements, health-conscious, wider demographic
- Cantu = ethnic hair care, so different demographic entirely

So the approach is:
1. Start with the base — everyone who shops at Clicks/Dis-Chem in pharmacy/beauty
2. Profile them — age, income, gender, province, what else they buy
3. Then slice differently per brand based on the price point and product type

Your point about affluence bias is valid — don't hardcode income as a filter for all of them. Use it as a differentiator between brands. Revlon buyer is different from La Roche-Posay buyer.

We already have the data for this in int_customer_category_spend and stg_customers. Check what CATEGORY_TWO values exist that relate to health/beauty/pharmacy:

SELECT DISTINCT CATEGORY_TWO FROM `fmn-sandbox.analytics.int_customer_category_spend` WHERE UPPER(CATEGORY_TWO) LIKE '%HEALTH%' OR UPPER(CATEGORY_TWO) LIKE '%PHARM%' OR UPPER(CATEGORY_TWO) LIKE '%BEAUTY%' OR UPPER(CATEGORY_TWO) LIKE '%CARE%'

Start there and we can build the audiences together.
