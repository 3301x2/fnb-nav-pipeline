Segment donut

We clustered 5.79M customers into 5 behavioural segments using k-means on 9 features — recency, frequency, spend, merchant diversity, category spread, weekend patterns. The model found these groups naturally in the data.

Champions (8.2%) — top spenders, most active, 1-day recency, 128 merchants. 8% of customers but ~45% of revenue.

Loyal (14.8%) — consistent spenders, 6-day recency, 73 merchants.

Mid-Tier (30.1%) — moderate spend, 14-day recency, 28 merchants. Biggest upsell opportunity.

At Risk (19.9%) — used to be active, 247-day recency. Re-engagement targets.

Dormant (27%) — lowest activity. 27% of people, under 5% of revenue.

Revenue concentration chart

This is the Pareto effect. Champions are 8% of people but 45% of revenue. Dormant are 27% of people but under 5% of revenue. The pitch to advertisers: targeting Champions gives you disproportionate return on every rand spent.

Churn risk

We built a logistic regression model that scores every customer's probability of going inactive. 666K customers — 12% of the base — are Critical or High risk. That's R31.6B in annual spend at risk. Even a 10% recovery = R3.16B saved.

What this page does

This is the dealmaker's page. Pick any category and client from the dropdowns — every chart updates instantly. In a pitch meeting you open this, select the client, and their entire profile is in front of you. What used to take 3 hours of manual SQL now takes one dropdown change.

KPIs — what each means

Customers — how many FNB cardholders shop at this store

Total spend — total card spend at this store (last 12 months)

Market share — this store's spend as % of total category spend

Penetration — % of all category customers who shop here

Avg transaction — average basket size per visit

Spend/customer — annual spend per customer at this store

Rank — position vs all competitors by total spend

Share of wallet — of a customer's total category spend, how much goes here

Competitor bars

The gold bar is the selected client. Dark bars are competitors. This instantly shows where the client sits. Example pitch line: "You're #1 in market share but #3 in spend per customer — your customers come often but spend less per visit than competitors. There's an upsell opportunity."

Internal vs External toggle

Internal shows real competitor names — for our team only. External anonymizes to "Competitor #1, #2, #3". We use external when presenting to clients — we can't reveal competitor data by name.

Store loyalty

Shows what % of a store's customers give it 50%+ of their category spend. High = sticky customers. Low = they're splitting across many stores.

Time patterns

When customers shop — morning, midday, afternoon, evening, weekend. Useful for ad scheduling.

Example: "Your customers shop Friday evenings — that's when to run your digital campaign for maximum reach."

Demographics + Geography

Age, gender, income distribution, and provincial spend — all filtered to the selected category. This tells the advertiser WHO their customers are and WHERE they are.

Segment profiles table

Each row is a customer segment. The columns show average behaviours. Champions: R771k avg spend, 1,570 transactions, 1-day recency, 128 merchants. Dormant: R11k spend, 35 transactions, 45+ day recency, 8 merchants. The separation is massive — these are genuinely different customer types.

Retention curve

Shows what % of customers are still active after N months. Starts at 100%, typically drops to ~38% by month 12.

This tells advertisers how long their audience stays engaged. "After 6 months, 55% of your customers are still active — that's strong retention and means your campaign ROI sustains over time."

Spend momentum

Accelerating — spending more recently (growing customers)

Steady — consistent spend

Slowing — still active but declining

Declining — significant drop, needs intervention

The urgency score tells you who to act on first. Declining + high historical spend = call them tomorrow. These are customers actively walking away.

The headline story

666K customers at Critical or High churn risk, representing R31.6B in spend. The model uses 15 features to predict who's about to leave. 83% accuracy on validation data.

Churn drivers chart

Shows WHY customers churn — long inactivity, dropping frequency, declining merchant diversity, spend drops. This is the explainability layer.

This is gold for retention. "These 178K customers are churning because their visit frequency dropped — send them a comeback offer targeting their peak shopping day."

CLV tiers

Platinum — highest predicted future value. Protect at all costs.

Gold/Silver — strong value, good upsell targets.

Bronze/Basic — lower value, mass campaigns only.

The killer insight: cross-reference CLV with churn. Platinum CLV + Critical churn = red alert. These are your most valuable customers about to leave. That's the list you action first.

Health scorecard

Each row is a spending category — customers, spend, growth %, churn %, Champion %, Dormant %, leader, health status.

If Rory asks "which categories should we focus on?" — look at growth + low churn + high Champion %. Those are healthy categories worth pitching to advertisers. Declining + high Dormant % = categories losing customers.

Cross-sell affinity (lift)

Lift 3.2x = customers who buy Groceries are 3.2 times more likely to also buy Clothing than a random customer.

Pitch angle: "If you're advertising Clothing, we can target Grocery shoppers — they're 3.2x more likely to cross-shop. That's a lookalike audience built from real transaction data, not interest guesses."

Pitch opportunities

Ranked by composite score combining market share, addressable market, gap to leader.

The dealmaking team uses this to prioritize outreach. "Nike scores 87 — large addressable market, low current share, big gap to the leader. High probability they'll want our data to close that gap."

Questions he'll probably ask

How fresh is this data?
Last 12 months of transaction data. The exact date range shows on the Overview page. Data refreshes when we re-run the pipeline — currently manual, can be scheduled daily if needed.
Can we filter by date range?
Not in the HTML version — it's a point-in-time snapshot. The Looker Studio version being built will have date filters. For now, we can regenerate with different date windows by changing a parameter.
How much did this cost?
About R700 in BigQuery compute for the initial full run across 2.2 billion transactions. Daily refreshes would be under R1. The dashboard itself is a static HTML file — zero hosting cost.
Can dealmakers use this themselves?
Yes. Open the HTML, pick category and client from dropdowns, everything updates live in the browser. No BigQuery access needed. Share on Teams or a shared drive.
How do we activate audiences?
Audience member lists are in BigQuery. Push to LiveRamp, which matches to Meta/Google/TikTok ad accounts. We have LiveRamp access — next step is confirming identity matching with their team.
Can we brand this for First Advertising?
Already built in. Drop in the logo and hex color codes, run one command, entire dashboard rebrands — header, charts, footer, everything. Two minutes.
What about POPIA?
No individual customer data leaves BigQuery. Dashboard shows aggregated stats only — segment averages, category totals, demographic distributions. No names, no account numbers. Audience activation via LiveRamp uses hashed identifiers — POPIA compliant by design.
How accurate is the ML?
K-means: Davies-Bouldin 1.295 (under 2.0 = well separated). Churn: 83.1% accuracy. Segments show clear separation — Champions R771k avg spend vs Dormant R11k. These aren't arbitrary groups — the model found genuine behavioural clusters.
Can we do brand-level like Revlon?
Data is at store + category level, not brand/product. But we proxy: Clicks Health & Beauty shoppers, filtered by demographics matching the brand's target. That's what the Clicks report shows. It's the best anyone can do without SKU-level data.
When is Looker Studio ready?
Simphiwe is building from a step-by-step guide. All 18 data sources are created in BigQuery. ETA end of next week. The HTML dashboard is the working deliverable right now.
Can this be shown at the Google Event?
Absolutely. Once Nhlanhla sends the brand assets and colours, one command rebrands everything. Takes 2 minutes to regenerate.
